<h3><strong><em>Competitions and Awards</em></strong></h3>
<p><strong>External Competitions</strong>
</p>
<p>Our school places strong emphasis on providing students with opportunities
to showcase their talents. As such, students are encouraged to take part
in various external activities and competitions. Through these platforms,
students have the opportunity to interact and learn from peers from different
schools, while building confidence and developing their skills.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Mother%20Tongue/Competitions%20and%20Awards/B%20Collage%2002.jpg">
</div>
<p><strong>Chinese Text Recitation Competition for Primary and Secondary Schools</strong>
</p>
<p>To strengthen students’ Chinese reading and recitation skills, spark interest
and enthusiasm for learning Chinese, and deepen their understanding of
texts. Our school sent students to participate in the <em>Chinese Text Recitation Competition for Primary and Secondary Schools</em> organised
by the Committee to Promote Chinese Language Learning. Our students also
benefited greatly from participating in online recitation guidance sessions
conducted by the Singapore Chinese Language Teachers’ Recitation Arts Association.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>2024 North Zone Primary and Secondary Schools Speech Competition</strong>
</p>
<p>Students from our school took part in this year’s North Zone Speech Competition,
where they shared their learning experiences through three-minute recorded
speeches. Teo Suan Ghee(4S) from Primary 4 delivered an outstanding performance
in the preliminary round and was awarded the <em>Potential Award</em>.</p>

<p><strong>2023 Chinese Text Recitation Competition for Primary and Secondary Schools</strong>
</p>
<p>Our school’s students participated in this year’s North Zone Speech Competition,
where they shared their learning and life experiences through recorded
speeches. They spoke with sincerity and confidence, delivered outstanding
performances, and achieved excellent results.</p>

<p><strong>2022 International Primary School Computer Creative Writing Competition</strong>
</p>
<p>To provide students with opportunities to develop their creative thinking
through online composition writing, our students took part in the International
Primary School Computer Creative Writing Competition. Students from countries
like Taiwan, China (including Hong Kong), Malaysia, Vietnam and Indonesia
also participated in this competition.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Mother%20Tongue/Competitions%20and%20Awards/A-Mao%20Ruihan-edited02.jpg">
</div>
<p>Mao Ruihan from 5 Solidarity demonstrated strong creativity in the <em>Story Relay</em> and <em>Picture-Based Writing</em> categories.
Showcasing clear thinking and originality, Ruihan stood out among numerous
participants and was awarded both the <strong>National Silver Award</strong> and
the <strong>International Silver Award</strong>.</p>
